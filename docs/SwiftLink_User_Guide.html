1. System Overview

1.1 Introduction

SwiftLink is a privacy-preserving fleet operations and communications platform designed for small corporate fleet operators in Kenya. It enables fleet managers to book, dispatch, and monitor trips on behalf of corporate clients, while providing drivers and clients with a secure, ephemeral channel to communicate during a trip — without either party ever exposing personal contact details to the other.

The platform is delivered as a Progressive Web App (PWA), making it installable on Android and iOS devices without requiring an app store download. All three user roles access the system through a single web URL, with the interface adapting to each role after authentication.

1.2 User Roles

RoleWho they areHow they access the systemDefault landing page
Fleet ManagerOperations staff responsible for dispatching drivers and managing the fleetEmail magic link (passwordless)/manager/dispatch
DriverDrivers employed by the fleet operatorUsername and password/driver/trips
ClientCorporate employees being transported on company businessSingle-use email link sent per booking/booking

1.3 Accessing the System

SwiftLink is accessed through a web browser. No software installation is required, though drivers and clients may install it as a PWA for a native-app experience:

  • Android (Chrome): Tap the browser menu → "Add to Home Screen"
  • iOS (Safari): Tap the Share icon → "Add to Home Screen"
Note: Installing as a PWA is optional. The full system works in any modern mobile or desktop browser without installation.

2.1 Logging In (Magic Link)

SwiftLink uses passwordless email authentication for fleet managers. No password is ever created or stored.

  1. 1Navigate to the SwiftLink login page and select the Manager tab.
  2. 2Enter your registered work email address and click "Send Sign-In Link".
  3. 3Check your inbox for an email from SwiftLink with the subject "Sign in to SwiftLink". It arrives within seconds.
  4. 4Click "Sign in to SwiftLink" in the email. You are taken directly to the Dispatch dashboard — no password required.
Caution: The sign-in link is single-use and expires after 15 minutes. If it has expired, return to the login page and enter your email again to receive a fresh link.
Privacy: Magic link authentication is used in place of passwords. No credential is ever created or stored on the system.

2.3 Creating a Trip Booking

Trip bookings are created by the fleet manager on behalf of a corporate client.

  1. 1From the Dispatch page, click "+ New Booking" in the top-right corner. A modal form appears.
  2. 2Enter the client's first name and corporate email address. Only the first name is stored in the trip record — no surname, no phone number.
  3. 3Enter the pickup location and destination.
  4. 4Set the pickup date and time.
  5. 5(Optional) Enter a flight number if this is an airport pickup or drop-off. The driver sees this on their trip card.
  6. 6(Optional) Enter special instructions — notes visible only to the assigned driver (e.g. "VIP delegation — display name card on arrival").
  7. 7Review the "Send booking access link" toggle. When ON (default), the client receives an email with a magic link to their personal trip dashboard. Set to OFF for internal trips where the client does not need app access.
  8. 8Click "Create Booking". The booking appears immediately in the Incoming Bookings panel with status Pending.
Privacy: The client's corporate email is used only to deliver their access link and trip status notifications. It is never visible to the assigned driver at any point.
[Figure 2.3 — New Booking Modal]

2.4 Assigning a Driver and Vehicle

Once a booking is created, the fleet manager assigns an available driver and vehicle to it.

  1. 1In the Incoming Bookings panel on the Dispatch page, locate the pending booking card.
  2. 2Review the trip details: client first name, route, pickup time, and any special instructions.
  3. 3Select an available driver from the driver dropdown.
  4. 4Select a vehicle from the vehicle dropdown. The vehicle make, model, and registration plate are shown to the client once the driver accepts.
  5. 5(Optional) Enter an ETA — the estimated arrival time at the pickup point. This is shown to the client as a reference time.
  6. 6Click "Assign".

What happens after assignment:

  • The booking status changes from Pending to Accepted.
  • The driver receives a push notification directing them to open the app and review the assignment.
  • The booking moves to the Awaiting Acceptance column. A timer bar shows how long the assignment has been waiting — it turns amber after 10 minutes and red after 20 minutes.
  • Once the driver accepts, the client receives a push notification with the driver's first name and vehicle details.
Caution: If the timer bar turns red, follow up with the driver directly or reassign the trip to another available driver. Drivers cannot be prompted again from the platform — contact must be made out-of-band.

Force-Completing a Trip

This action is available for exceptional cases only — for example, when a driver is unreachable and cannot complete the trip themselves.

  1. 1Locate the trip row in the Active Trips panel.
  2. 2Click "Complete". An inline confirm/cancel prompt appears.
  3. 3Click "Confirm" to proceed.

The trip is marked completed, both driver and client session keys are immediately destroyed from the server, and the client enters a 24-hour complaint window.

Caution: Force-complete permanently destroys the session. The action cannot be undone. Use it only when normal trip completion is not possible.

2.6 Managing Drivers

Navigate to Drivers from the pill navigation bar.

Adding a New Driver

  1. 1Click "Add Driver".
  2. 2Enter the driver's full name, employee ID, and a password they will use to log in.
  3. 3Click "Save". The driver appears in the roster and can now log in on their device.

Deactivating a Driver

A deactivated driver cannot log in and will not appear in the assignment dropdown on the Dispatch page. Their historical trip records are preserved. To deactivate, click "Deactivate" on the driver row and confirm.

Driver Availability

Availability is set automatically by the system: a driver becomes unavailable the moment they accept a trip and available again once the trip is completed.

2.8 Privacy Dashboard and Session Monitor

Navigate to Privacy Dashboard (labelled Dashboard in the nav) from the pill navigation bar.

What a Session Is

When a driver accepts a trip, SwiftLink creates two cryptographic session keys in Redis (an in-memory data store) — one for the driver and one for the client. These keys:

  • Authenticate both parties to the secure chat relay — messages cannot be sent without a valid session key
  • Define the TTL (Time-To-Live), after which session data is automatically deleted
  • Are destroyed immediately and permanently when the driver completes the trip

This architecture ensures that all chat data is ephemeral by design — it exists only for the duration of the trip and cannot be recovered afterwards.

Reading the Session Monitor

TTL Badge ColourMeaning
GreenMore than 50% of session time remaining — trip is in early stage
Amber25–50% remaining — trip is likely nearing completion
RedLess than 25% remaining — session will expire soon

Each row in the monitor shows the trip ID, client and driver TTL badges, and the complaint window TTL (24 hours from completion, counting down live).

The Destruction Proof Log

Every session destruction event is written to the audit log with a timestamp and a SHA-256 destruction hash. This hash is computed from the session content at the moment of deletion and serves as cryptographic evidence that the session was properly wiped — not merely abandoned or hidden. The Destruction Proof Log panel on the Privacy Dashboard displays these entries in a filterable table.

Privacy: This dashboard provides demonstrable evidence of compliance with the data minimisation and storage limitation principles of data protection law. No session data persists beyond trip completion.

2.9 Managing Complaints

Navigate to Complaints from the pill navigation bar. A red notification badge on the nav item shows the number of open complaints.

Complaint Statuses

StatusMeaning
OpenFiled by the client — no investigation started yet
Under InvestigationInvestigation active — encrypted message archive is accessible
ResolvedInvestigation complete — no further status changes permitted

Starting an Investigation

  1. 1Click a complaint row to expand the full complaint detail card.
  2. 2Use the status dropdown to select "Under Investigation".
  3. 3The "View Messages" button becomes active. Click it to decrypt and read the message archive from the trip.
  4. 4Review the conversation between the driver and client.
  5. 5Record your findings in the investigation notes field (saved automatically).
  6. 6Click "Notify Driver" if you need to formally inform the driver that a review is in progress.
  7. 7When complete, use the dropdown to select "Resolved".

If you need to pause mid-investigation, use the dropdown to return the status to Open. The message archive becomes inaccessible again until the investigation is restarted.

Privacy: The message archive is AES-256-GCM encrypted at rest. It can only be decrypted while the complaint status is Under Investigation. This restriction is enforced at the server level — the server refuses to return the archive in any other state, regardless of what the client sends.

3.1 Logging In (Password)

Driver accounts are created by the fleet manager, who sets the initial credentials. Drivers log in with an employee ID and password.

  1. 1Navigate to the SwiftLink login page and select the Driver tab.
  2. 2Enter your employee ID and password as provided by your fleet manager.
  3. 3Tap "Sign In". You are taken to your Trips list.
Caution: If you cannot log in, contact your fleet manager. They can reset your credentials from the Drivers management page.

The Trips page shows all trips assigned to your account. Each trip card displays a status badge that summarises what action (if any) is required.

3.3 Accepting a Trip

  1. 1Read the full trip card: client first name, pickup location, destination, pickup time, and assigned vehicle.
  2. 2If a purple note box is present on the card, read it carefully — it contains special instructions from your fleet manager (e.g. flight numbers, VIP notes).
  3. 3Tap "Accept".

When you accept:

  • The trip moves to In Progress.
  • A secure chat session opens — you and the client can now communicate.
  • The client receives a push notification showing your first name and their vehicle details.
  • Your status becomes On Trip in the fleet manager's dashboard — you will not appear in the assignment dropdown for other trips.
Privacy: Once you accept, the client can see your first name, vehicle make, model, and registration plate. No other personal details are shared with them at any point.

3.4 Declining a Trip

  1. 1Tap "Decline" on an Assigned trip card.
  2. 2Enter a reason in the text field. This is required and cannot be left blank. Examples: "Vehicle mechanical issue", "Medical emergency", "Road closure on route".
  3. 3Tap "Confirm Decline".

The trip returns to the fleet manager for reassignment. Your decline reason is logged and reviewed by your manager — it is not shared with the client.

3.5 Active Trip — Chat and Completion

Tap "View Active Trip" on an In Progress card to open the full trip interface. This page shows your trip manifest at the top and the secure chat panel below.

Using the Secure Chat Channel

The Secure Channel (🔒) panel is the only authorised way to communicate with your client during a trip. You will not see the client's phone number or email at any point — all messages are relayed through SwiftLink's server so neither party's contact details are ever exposed.

  1. 1Type your message in the box at the bottom of the Secure Channel panel.
  2. 2Tap Send. The client receives the message in real time.
  3. 3If you briefly lose signal, any message you sent shows a ⏳ icon. It will be delivered automatically when your connection is restored. Keep the app open in the background.

Completing the Trip

When the client has been safely dropped off at their destination:

  1. 1Tap "Complete Trip — Dropped Off ✓".
  2. 2The trip ends immediately. Both your session and the client's session are destroyed on the server. The chat channel closes.
  3. 3The client enters a 24-hour window during which they can file a complaint if needed.
Privacy: After completion, all message content is permanently deleted from the server — unless the client files a complaint within 24 hours. In that case, the archive is preserved solely for the fleet manager's investigation, then deleted on resolution.

3.6 Profile and Push Notifications

Navigate to Profile from the bottom navigation bar.

Your Profile Details

Your full name and employee ID are set by your fleet manager when your account is created. If any details are incorrect, contact your fleet manager to have them updated — you cannot edit these yourself.

Enabling Push Notifications

Push notifications alert you when a new trip has been assigned to you, or when a trip has been cancelled by the client.

  1. 1Tap "Enable Notifications" on your profile page.
  2. 2Your browser shows a permission prompt — tap "Allow".
  3. 3The toggle turns on. You will receive alerts even when the app is in the background or the screen is off.
Note: Push notification permissions are tied to your specific browser and device. If you switch to a new phone or a different browser, you will need to enable notifications again from the Profile page.

If you denied permission by mistake: Open your browser settings, find SwiftLink in the site permissions list, change Blocked to Allowed, then return to the Profile page and tap "Enable Notifications" again.

Client Guide
Accessing your booking, tracking your trip, communicating with your driver, filing complaints, and viewing booking history

4.1 Receiving and Opening Your Booking Link

Clients do not create accounts on SwiftLink. Access is granted per booking via a single-use link sent to your corporate email address when your fleet manager creates your trip.

  1. 1Check your inbox for an email from SwiftLink with the subject "Your SwiftLink booking link".
  2. 2Open the email and tap or click the link in the message body.
  3. 3You are taken directly to your personal trip dashboard — no password required. The link signs you in automatically on first open.
Caution: The link is single-use and expires after 15 minutes if unused. If it has expired, contact your fleet manager to resend it. Check your spam or junk folder if you cannot find the email.
Privacy: Your email address is used only to deliver your booking link and send trip status notifications. It is never shared with your driver. Your driver sees only your first name.

What Your Email Is Used For

  • Delivering your booking access link
  • Sending trip status updates (driver assigned, trip complete)
  • Allowing you to recover access to your booking history
[Figure 4.1 — Booking Landing Page (Booking Form State)]

4.2 Booking Confirmed — Awaiting Driver Assignment

After opening your link, if no driver has been assigned yet, your trip dashboard shows a Pending status. This means your booking has been received and your fleet manager is currently assigning a driver and vehicle.

  • You do not need to do anything at this stage.
  • The page updates automatically when a driver is assigned.
  • You may receive a push notification the moment your driver is confirmed — enable notifications on the prompt shown on your screen.
Caution: If your pickup time is approaching and no driver has been assigned, contact your fleet manager directly.
[Figure 4.2 — Trip Pending State]
Trip dashboard showing Pending status badge and "Awaiting driver assignment" message

4.3 Driver Assigned — Before the Trip Starts

Once your fleet manager assigns a driver and they accept, your trip status changes to Accepted. Your dashboard updates to show:

  • Your driver's first name
  • Their vehicle — make, model, and registration plate
  • An ETA (estimated arrival time at your pickup point), if your fleet manager set one
Privacy: You will not see your driver's phone number. All communication goes through the secure in-app chat, which protects both you and the driver.

Messaging Your Driver

  1. 1Scroll down to the Secure Channel section on your trip page.
  2. 2Type your message in the text box and tap Send.
  3. 3Your driver receives it in real time. Use this to confirm pickup landmarks, share updates, or flag last-minute changes.

Cancelling Your Trip

You can cancel while the trip status is Pending or Accepted:

  1. 1Tap "Cancel Trip" on your trip page.
  2. 2Confirm the cancellation when prompted.
  3. 3Your fleet manager and driver (if already assigned) are notified automatically.
Caution: Cancellation is not available once the driver has started the journey (status In Progress). Contact your fleet manager directly in that case.
[Figure 4.3 — Driver Assigned State]
Trip dashboard showing driver card with first name, vehicle details, ETA badge, and Secure Channel panel

4.4 Trip in Progress

Once your driver confirms your pickup, the trip status changes to In Progress. The secure chat channel is open.

Communicating During the Trip

  1. 1Use the Secure Channel section on your screen to communicate with your driver.
  2. 2Type your message and tap Send.
  3. 3If your signal drops briefly, your message shows a ⏳ icon and will be delivered automatically when connectivity is restored.

If something goes wrong during the trip, use the chat to raise it with your driver first. If the issue cannot be resolved through chat, contact your fleet manager directly by phone.

What Happens to Your Data

The moment your driver taps "Complete Trip", the system destroys both your session and the driver's session on the server. Message content is permanently deleted at this point — unless you file a complaint within 24 hours, in which case the archive is preserved for your fleet manager's investigation only.

Privacy: SwiftLink is built on a privacy-by-design model. Trip sessions are ephemeral — data is not kept beyond what is strictly necessary for the operation.

4.5 Trip Ended — Filing a Complaint

When the trip is marked complete, your dashboard transitions to the post-trip view. You have up to 24 hours to file a complaint if something went wrong.

Filing a Complaint

  1. 1Scroll to the "File a Complaint" section on your trip page. A countdown timer shows how much time you have remaining.
  2. 2Select a complaint category from the dropdown (e.g. "Driver conduct", "Route deviation", "Vehicle condition").
  3. 3Describe what happened in the text field.
  4. 4Tap "Submit Complaint".

Your message history from the trip is preserved and your fleet manager is notified. You can track the investigation status on the same page as it progresses from Open to Under Investigation to Resolved.

Caution: After the 24-hour window closes, your message history is permanently deleted. If you intend to file a complaint, do not wait — act as soon as possible after the trip ends.

If the Trip Was Cancelled

If the trip was cancelled before it started, the booking session was closed at the point of cancellation. Contact your fleet manager if you need a new booking arranged.

[Figure 4.5 — Completed Trip — Complaint Section]

4.6 Accessing Your Booking History

To view a history of all your previous trips without having an active booking link, you can request a recovery link from your fleet manager, or use the "Access booking history" option on the booking page if you have an expired link.

What the History View Shows

  • All trips registered under your corporate email address
  • Pickup location and destination
  • Pickup date and time (displayed in East Africa Time)
  • Assigned driver (first name) and vehicle
  • Trip status

Limitations of the History View

History access is a temporary, read-only session valid for 2 hours from when you opened the recovery link. You cannot edit or cancel bookings from this view. To make changes to an upcoming booking, contact your fleet manager.

  • Message content from completed trips is not retained unless a complaint was filed — this is by design.
  • Cancelled trips appear in the list with their status — route and booking details are preserved.
Privacy: Your history session expires automatically after 2 hours. This limits the exposure of your booking records if you are using a shared or unattended device.
